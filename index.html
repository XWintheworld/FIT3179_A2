Environmental Effects

The graph shows a significant rise in sea level from 1880 to 2013. The rising sea level is mainly due to the meltwater from glaciers and ice sheets and the thermal expansion of seawater as it warms.

Low-lying islands and coastal communities are threatened by sea level rise, for instance, Japan. The low-lying area might be lower than the sea level, so some of the lands might be permanently lost to the sea.

Drivers of Recent Temperature Rise

Greenhouse gases are the most significant drivers of global climate change. The greenhouse gases that affect Earth include carbon dioxide, methane, water vapour, nitrous oxide, and ozone. The left bubble plot shows a positive relationship between CO2 emissions and tree cover loss. This positive relationship means that tree cover loss is one of the factors that causes CO2 increase. Luckily, Australia has less tree cover loss. However, climate change is a global issue, and all countries reduce deforestation.
